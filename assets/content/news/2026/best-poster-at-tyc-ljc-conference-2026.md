# Aritra Wins Runner-Up Best Poster Prize at TYC-LJC Conference 2026

We are thrilled to announce that **Aritra Roy**, a PhD student in our research group, was awarded **second prize** for his poster presentation at the inaugural **[Thomas Young Centre – Lennard-Jones Centre Materials and Modelling Conference](https://tyc-ljc.co.uk/)** held at Queen Mary University of London on **12th January 2026**.

## The Award-Winning Research

Aritra's poster, titled **"ComProScanner: A multi-agent based agile framework for high quality composition-property structured data extraction from scientific literature"**, stood out in a competitive field of approximately 30 entries. The research presents an innovative approach to automated data extraction from scientific publications, combining multi-agent systems with natural language processing to build high-quality structured databases for materials science research.

ComProScanner addresses a critical challenge in computational materials science: the efficient extraction and structuring of composition-property relationships from the vast scientific literature. This work demonstrates the potential of artificial intelligence to accelerate materials discovery by automating the labor-intensive process of literature data mining.

## About the Conference

The inaugural TYC-LJC Materials and Modelling Conference brought together approximately 75 delegates from leading UK universities including Queen Mary University of London, University College London, Imperial College London, King's College London, London South Bank University, Brunel University, University of Cambridge, and University of Oxford.

The conference provided a vibrant platform for researchers across career stages to present their work, exchange ideas, and foster collaborations in computational materials science and molecular modelling.
