# SLIMES Lab Hosts MCC-VASP Workshop at LSBU

We are delighted to announce that **Dr. John Buckeridge** successfully organized and ran the **2nd VASP-MCC Workshop** at London South Bank University from **19th to 21st January 2026**. This prestigious three-day event brought together novices, experienced users, developers, and experts to discuss applications of the widely used [VASP](https://www.vasp.at/) software for first-principles materials modelling.

## Distinguished Contributions

The workshop was honored by the participation of **[Professor Georg Kresse](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=Pn8ouvAAAAAJ&hl=en)**, the main developer of VASP and one of the top 5 most cited physicists of all time. Professor Kresse's contributions from the VASP development team provided participants with invaluable insights into the software that has become a cornerstone of computational materials science worldwide.

The event featured a keynote address from **[Professor Sir Richard Catlow FRS](https://scholar.google.co.uk/citations?user=eQRHK8EAAAAJ&hl=en)**, whose pioneering contributions to computational chemistry and catalysis research added significant depth to the workshop discussions. Additionally, **[Dr. Suela Kellici](https://researchportal.lsbu.ac.uk/en/persons/suela-kellici/)**, a member of the MADD Group, kindly delivered an invited talk sharing her research expertise.

## Workshop Structure and Activities

The workshop was designed to cater to a diverse audience ranging from newcomers to seasoned practitioners in computational materials modelling. The program included theoretical presentations, practical demonstrations, and collaborative discussions on advanced VASP techniques.

A hands-on session was expertly run by industrial partners **[Materials Design](https://www.materialsdesign.com/)**, providing participants with practical experience in applying VASP to real-world materials challenges. This industry perspective complemented the academic presentations and gave attendees a comprehensive view of VASP applications across different sectors.

## Attendance and Impact

The workshop attracted over **55 delegates** from across the UK and beyond, reflecting the strong interest in advanced computational materials science techniques. The diverse participant background fostered rich discussions and knowledge exchange between researchers at different career stages and from various institutional settings.

## Social Engagement

The workshop included a lively social event at **[The Real Greek, Bankside](https://www.therealgreek.com/restaurant/bankside/)** on the evening of 20th January. This informal gathering provided an excellent opportunity for participants to network, discuss research ideas, and build collaborative relationships in a relaxed atmosphere. The social dimension of the workshop proved invaluable for strengthening connections within the UK computational materials science community.

## Significance for LSBU

Hosting this workshop at [London South Bank University](https://www.lsbu.ac.uk/) represents an important milestone for the institution's growing reputation in computational materials science. The event showcased LSBU's commitment to advancing research in materials modelling and provided a platform for our researchers to engage with leading experts in the field.

The workshop aligns closely with ongoing research activities within the SLIMES Research Group, particularly in areas such as machine learning for materials discovery, piezoelectric materials prediction, and high-throughput computational screening.

## Looking Forward

The success of this 2nd VASP-MCC Workshop demonstrates the vital role that such gatherings play in advancing computational materials science. The knowledge shared, connections forged, and collaborations initiated during these three days will continue to benefit the research community for years to come.

We extend our sincere gratitude to Dr. John Buckeridge for his excellent organization, to all speakers and contributors for their insights, and to every participant who made this workshop a memorable and productive event.
